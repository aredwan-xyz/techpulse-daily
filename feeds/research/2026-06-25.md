## 🎯 The Core Idea  
This paper introduces RevengeBench, a tool that helps developers reconstruct the decision-making code of AI agents by observing their behavior in gaming environments. It enhances this process by allowing the design of controlled experiments to better understand how these agents make decisions.

## 🔍 The Problem They Solved  
Previously, researchers struggled to decipher the internal logic of AI systems just by watching their behavior, which made it hard to understand their decision-making processes. This ambiguity hindered the improvement and development of more effective AI systems, as the “black box” nature of their decision-making was poorly understood.

## 💡 The Key Innovation  
RevengeBench allows a learner to observe the actions of an AI agent in games and create tailored experimental conditions to probe deeper into its decision-making process. By utilizing a benchmark of various game environments and policies, it demonstrates that structured experimentation can enhance the accuracy of reconstructing the underlying code that governs AI behavior.

## 🛠️ Why Developers Should Care  
RevengeBench provides developers with new methodologies to dissect and understand AI decision-making, paving the way for more transparent and interpretable AI systems. With clearer insights into how agents function, developers can refine their models more effectively, leading to improved overall performance and reliability.

## ⚡ TL;DR  
RevengeBench is a new benchmark that helps developers figure out how AI agents make decisions by analyzing their behavior and conducting controlled experiments.
