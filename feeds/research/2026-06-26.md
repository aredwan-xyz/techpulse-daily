## 🎯 The Core Idea
This paper examines how advanced voice AI systems interpret spoken language by analyzing both the words said and the emotional tone behind them. It reveals that these systems can recognize emotions but often choose to disregard them when making decisions.

## 🔍 The Problem They Solved
Before this research, it was unclear how effectively current AI voice systems could interpret emotional cues in speech alongside actual content. The systems were known to interact based on verbal input, but their handling of vocal delivery and emotional context could lead to poor outcomes in real-world scenarios.

## 💡 The Key Innovation
The authors highlight a critical oversight: while these AI systems can understand emotions when directly queried, they fail to incorporate that understanding into their operational decisions. This points to a gap between perception and action within voice AIs, suggesting that emotional intelligence should be integrated into decision-making processes to improve interactions.

## 🛠️ Why Developers Should Care
For developers building AI voice systems, this research underscores the importance of enhancing emotional awareness in AI interactions. By ensuring that systems acknowledge vocal delivery—like tone and emotion—their effectiveness and user satisfaction can be dramatically improved, especially in scenarios involving sensitive human interactions.

## ⚡ TL;DR
Many voice AI systems hear emotions but don't listen to them, often leading to insensitive decisions in critical moments.

## 📝 Original Abstract

> Speech conveys information through both words and vocal delivery. We evaluate four leading production realtime voice systems-OpenAI's GPT Realtime 2, Google's Gemini 3.1 Flash Live, and Alibaba's Qwen3.5 Omni Plus and Omni Flash-on tasks where the words and the delivery patterns both convey meaningful information. Across three consequential scenarios, all four systems act on the words rather than the voice. They end calls with crying callers who insist nothing is wrong, approve wire transfers authorized in frightened voices, and enroll callers whose agreement is clearly sarcastic.
