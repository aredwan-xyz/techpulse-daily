# Developer Market Pulse – Sunday, June 07, 2026

## 🔥 Hottest Skills Right Now
1. **Generative AI** - As businesses continue to integrate AI into their daily operations, skills in generative models (like GPT variants) are at a premium. Companies are seeking experts who can create compelling text, visuals, and even code.

2. **Cloud-Native Development** - With the expansion of cloud services, skills in building microservices using platforms like Kubernetes and Docker are highly sought after. Employers are looking for developers who can optimize cloud solutions.

3. **Data Science with Python and R** - The demand for data-driven decisions has made data science skills indispensable, particularly proficiency in Python and R for data analysis and machine learning.

4. **Blockchain Development** - Interest in decentralized applications (dApps) and cryptocurrencies is surging. Developers proficient in Ethereum and smart contract programming are in high demand.

5. **Cybersecurity Expertise** - With cyber threats on the rise, individual roles specializing in security (especially those adapting to AI) are becoming essential across tech companies.

## 💰 Salary Signals
1. **AI/ML Engineer**: Average compensation is now hitting $145,000, a 12% increase compared to last year, reflecting the technology's expanding role in various sectors.
   
2. **Full-Stack Developer**: Average salaries have risen to around $120,000, attributed to the increased demand for versatile developers capable of handling both front-end and back-end tasks.

3. **Data Scientist**: The average salary for data scientists has climbed to $135,000, with top-tier companies offering upwards of $160,000 for candidates with experience in advanced analytics and AI integration.

## 🚀 Roles in High Demand
1. **AI/ML Engineer** - The explosion of generative models and AI applications has created a surge in open positions, with HN threads showing over 500 companies hiring for this role consistently.

2. **Cloud Solutions Architect** - The transition to cloud infrastructures has led to more businesses seeking specialists who can design scalable systems. Open positions are now in the range of 700.

3. **Cybersecurity Analyst** - As security breaches become increasingly common, the need for analysts has spiked, with more than 600 roles available. Companies recognize that robust security measures are pivotal for business continuity.

## 📉 What's Cooling Off
1. **Traditional Mobile App Development** - While mobile app development remains important, specific skills like native iOS and Android development are starting to plateau in demand, as hybrid frameworks are increasingly adopted.

2. **SEO Specialists** - With search algorithms evolving towards AI-driven outcomes, traditional SEO roles are declining in demand. Companies prefer candidates who can integrate SEO with content creation and data analysis.

## 🌍 Remote Landscape
The remote work landscape is stabilizing, with about 70% of tech companies offering flexible hybrid models. Interestingly, fully remote roles have decreased slightly as companies seek to foster in-office collaboration while still accommodating flexibility.

## 💡 Career Move of the Week
Consider enhancing your skills in **prompt engineering** for AI tools. This emerging skill is crucial for optimizing interaction with AI systems, particularly as generative AI becomes more pervasive in various fields. Many companies are looking for developers who can effectively craft prompts to maximize AI capabilities.
