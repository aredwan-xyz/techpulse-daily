# Developer Market Pulse - July 10, 2026

As we head into the summer, the tech job market remains dynamic and competitive, particularly for developers and AI professionals. Here’s a snapshot of the current landscape to keep you informed.

## 🔥 Hottest Skills Right Now
1. **Generative AI**: With the increasing use of AI content generation models, professionals skilled in machine learning frameworks (like TensorFlow and PyTorch) tailored for generative models are in high demand.
2. **Blockchain Development**: Continued interest in decentralized finance (DeFi) and NFTs has fueled the need for developers skilled in Solidity and smart contract audits.
3. **Cloud Native Technologies**: Skills in Kubernetes, Docker, and serverless architecture are essential as companies shift towards cloud-native environments to enhance scalability.
4. **Data Engineering**: Proficiency with ETL processes, data pipeline orchestration (e.g., Apache Airflow), and tools like Apache Spark is critical as businesses aim to harness big data effectively.
5. **DevOps Practices**: Strong demand for developers who can bridge the gap between development and operations, focusing on CI/CD practices, infrastructure as code (e.g., Terraform), and agile methodologies.

## 💰 Salary Signals
- **AI/ML Engineers**: The average salary has risen to $165,000, with top talent in major tech hubs earning upwards of $210,000.
- **Full-Stack Developers**: The average compensation sits at $135,000, showing a robust increase due to high demand for versatile skill sets.
- **DevOps Engineers**: Average salaries have climbed to $140,000, reflecting the increasing importance of efficiency in deployment processes.

## 🚀 Roles in High Demand
1. **Machine Learning Engineer**: Companies are leveraging AI more than ever; there are currently over 25,000 openings nationwide, as businesses look to develop intelligent applications.
2. **DevOps Engineer**: With more than 18,000 roles available, organizations are prioritizing integration and deployment efficiency as critical business functions.
3. **Full-Stack Developer**: The demand remains high at around 20,000 open positions, particularly for those proficient in both front and backend technologies.

## 📉 What's Cooling Off
- **Java Developers**: Demand for Java developers is showing signs of decline as companies increasingly favor more modern languages and frameworks for web and mobile app development.
- **Legacy System Experts**: Roles focused solely on outdated technologies like COBOL are dwindling, as companies invest in upgrading to modern systems.

## 🌍 Remote Landscape
Remote work in tech continues to be a cornerstone of employment strategies. Approximately 70% of tech companies are offering fully remote or hybrid positions, with many candidates specifically seeking roles that provide location flexibility. Major cities like San Francisco and NYC are seeing candidates favoring remote opportunities, pushing employers to adapt their strategies accordingly. 

## 💡 Career Move of the Week
Consider diversifying your skill set by investing time in generative AI technologies. Online courses and certifications are available through platforms like Coursera and Udacity, which can bolster your marketability in an evolving field.
