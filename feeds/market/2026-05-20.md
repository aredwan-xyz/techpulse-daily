## Market Pulse - May 20, 2026

### 🔥 Hottest Skills Right Now
1. **Generative AI**: There has been an explosive demand for developers skilled in generative models, especially in fields such as content creation and design. Companies are seeking expertise in frameworks like GPT and DALL-E.
2. **MLOps**: As organizations strive to streamline the deployment of AI/ML models into production, MLOps has emerged as a must-have skill. Proficiency in tools like Kubeflow and MLflow is highly sought after.
3. **Serverless Architecture**: With the transition to more agile development practices, serverless computing skills, particularly in AWS Lambda and Azure Functions, have become crucial for efficient backend development.
4. **Cross-Platform Mobile Development**: Developer experience with frameworks like React Native and Flutter has surged as companies look for cost-effective solutions to deploy applications across platforms without sacrificing performance.
5. **Cybersecurity for AI Systems**: As AI solutions proliferate, the need for cybersecurity expertise specifically tailored to protect AI systems is increasingly important, marking a crucial intersection of AI and security.

### 💰 Salary Signals
- **AI Engineers**: The median salary for AI engineers has reached $150,000, reflecting a 10% increase over the past year largely due to heightened demand in both startups and large enterprises.
- **Full-Stack Developers**: Average compensation for full-stack developers is now averaging around $120,000, indicating a steady growth in salaries, especially for those with skills in modern frameworks.
- **MLOps Specialists**: The entry-level salary for MLOps roles has jumped to $115,000, as businesses recognize the value of operationalizing machine learning.

### 🚀 Roles in High Demand
1. **Machine Learning Engineer**: With a growing need for data-driven solutions, there are over 15,000 open positions for ML engineers, primarily in tech startups and large enterprises aiming to integrate AI into their products.
2. **Cloud Solutions Architect**: More than 12,000 job listings are currently available for this role, driven by the shift to cloud-native solutions and the increased focus on multi-cloud strategies.
3. **Full-Stack Developer**: The demand remains high with over 20,000 positions open as organizations continue to digitalize their services and require versatile developers who can handle both front-end and back-end tasks.

### 📉 What's Cooling Off
- **Legacy Technologies**: Skills related to older enterprise systems, such as COBOL and mainframe programming, are seeing reduced demand as companies modernize their tech stacks. 
- **Basic Front-End Frameworks**: Skills centered around older front-end libraries and frameworks like AngularJS are losing traction, with a growing emphasis on more modern alternatives like Vue.js and React.

### 🌍 Remote Landscape
Remote work in tech continues to evolve, with 60% of new tech roles offering flexible remote options. However, companies are becoming more selective, favoring candidates based in certain geographical areas or time zones. This trend has led to an uptick in hybrid positions, where developers are expected to work in-office part-time.

### 💡 Career Move of the Week
**Upskill in Generative AI**: If you're looking to position yourself advantageously in the job market, consider diving into generative AI. Take an online course on the latest models and start building your own applications; showcasing projects can significantly boost your visibility to potential employers.
