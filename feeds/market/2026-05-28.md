# Developer Market Pulse - May 28, 2026

## 🔥 Hottest Skills Right Now
1. **Generative AI**: The demand for skills in generative AI has skyrocketed, as companies across industries are looking to leverage AI for content creation and automation. Proficiency in tools like DALL-E and language models such as GPT-4 is particularly sought after.
   
2. **MLOps**: The growth of ML models in production has propelled MLOps specialists to the forefront, with organizations prioritizing robust deployment practices. Knowledge of CI/CD for ML pipelines and tools like MLflow or Kubeflow is critical.

3. **Cybersecurity in AI**: As companies increasingly leverage AI, the need for security experts who can audit and protect AI systems has become essential. Skills in adversarial machine learning and secure architecture design are in high demand.

4. **Serverless Architectures**: With startups and enterprises leaning towards agile development, familiarity with serverless computing on platforms like AWS Lambda and Azure Functions is highly regarded.

5. **DevOps with Kubernetes Expertise**: Kubernetes continues to grow in popularity for container orchestration, and those skilled in deploying scalable applications on Kubernetes are seeing increased opportunities, especially in cloud-native environments.

## 💰 Salary Signals
1. **AI Engineer Average Salary**: Currently, AI engineers are seeing average salaries around $142,000, up 8% from last year, indicating strong demand for talent in this area.
   
2. **Full-Stack Developer Increase**: Full-stack developers now command an average salary of $120,000, reflecting a 5% increase attributed to their versatility in handling both front-end and back-end technologies.

3. **MLOps Engineer Premium**: MLOps engineers are earning approximately $130,000 on average, highlighting a 10% salary boost compared to the previous year, showcasing the premium companies place on expertise in deploying ML solutions effectively.

## 🚀 Roles in High Demand
1. **AI Software Engineer**: With the rapid advancements in AI technologies, this role leads the pack with 15,000+ open positions this week. Companies are keen on engineers who can develop and integrate AI solutions into products.

2. **Full-Stack Developer**: Still a staple in the job market, there are around 12,000+ openings for full-stack roles as businesses continue to evolve their digital platforms.

3. **Data Scientist**: With approximately 10,500 positions available, the need for data scientists who can analyze and model data towards informed business decisions is critical, especially in companies focusing on data-driven strategy.

## 📉 What's Cooling Off
1. **Traditional Web Development (HTML/CSS)**: Skills in traditional front-end frameworks like basic HTML/CSS are seeing a decline as companies look for developers with a greater emphasis on JavaScript frameworks or comprehensive full-stack capabilities.

2. **Legacy Technologies**: Roles emphasizing legacy systems and languages, such as COBOL or older Java frameworks, are declining as the industry shifts towards modern stacks and cloud-based solutions.

## 🌍 Remote Landscape
The remote work trend remains strong, with over 60% of job postings in the tech sector now offering remote or hybrid work options. Companies are attempting to balance in-person collaboration with remote flexibility, providing a wider range of candidates for open roles.

## 💡 Career Move of the Week
Consider upskilling in MLOps tools and frameworks, such as Kubeflow or Terraform, to remain competitive. With companies betting heavily on scalable ML solutions, gaining expertise in MLOps can enhance your employability and salary potential significantly.
