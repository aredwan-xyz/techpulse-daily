# Developer Market Pulse - June 25, 2026

## 🔥 Hottest Skills Right Now
1. **Generative AI Development**: As businesses increasingly adopt generative models for content creation, roles focusing on building and fine-tuning these models have skyrocketed in demand. Proficiency in tools like OpenAI’s API, TensorFlow, and PyTorch is crucial.

2. **Data Engineering**: With the explosion of data analytics in organizations, the need for professionals who can build scalable data infrastructure is critical. Skills in ETL processes, Apache Kafka, and cloud data warehousing continue to be at the forefront.

3. **Cybersecurity Engineering**: As cyber threats evolve, the demand for cybersecurity professionals with skills in threat hunting, penetration testing, and incident response has surged. Familiarity with frameworks such as NIST and MITRE ATT&CK is a valuable asset.

4. **DevOps & Continuous Integration/Continuous Deployment (CI/CD)**: The automation of deployment processes remains a key focus for firms, and skills in tools like Jenkins, Kubernetes, and Docker are in high demand.

5. **Full-Stack Development with Modern Frameworks**: Proficiency in frameworks like React, Angular, and Vue.js alongside backend technologies like Node.js and Python is crucial as companies look for versatile developers who can handle multiple parts of their web applications.

## 💰 Salary Signals
1. **AI/ML Roles**: The average salary for AI/ML Engineers has risen to approximately $140,000, up 12% from last year, reflecting the increasing value placed on their expertise in generative models and data algorithms.
  
2. **Cybersecurity Engineers**: Salaries in this sector have seen a healthy growth, with average wages now around $115,000, driven by the urgent need for organizations to secure their infrastructure.

3. **Full-Stack Developers**: Full-stack developers now command an average salary of $120,000, which marks an 8% increase year-over-year as companies prioritize flexible tech talent across their stacks.

## 🚀 Roles in High Demand
1. **AI/ML Engineer**: The surge in interest in artificial intelligence has led to a 20% increase in job postings for AI/ML engineers, driven by companies implementing AI solutions across industries.

2. **Data Engineer**: With more organizations shifting their focus to data-driven decision-making, data engineering roles are up by 15%, making it one of the most sought-after positions.

3. **Cybersecurity Analyst**: As cyber threats are becoming more sophisticated, the need for cybersecurity analysts has grown by 22%. Companies are investing heavily in building their cybersecurity teams to safeguard assets.

## 📉 What's Cooling Off
1. **Legacy IT Systems Support**: Roles focused on maintaining outdated systems are on the decline, with many organizations opting for cloud-based solutions and modern software alternatives.
   
2. **Frontend Development with Vanilla JavaScript**: While still important, there’s a noticeable decline in demand for vanilla JavaScript expertise alone as employers favor candidates with modern framework skills.

## 🌍 Remote Landscape
The remote work trend has stabilized, with about 64% of tech companies offering hybrid options. Interestingly, there's a renewed balance in candidate expectations for salary adjustments related to their remote work flexibility as some companies are starting to adjust compensation based on location again, prioritizing on-site presence for critical roles.

## 💡 Career Move of the Week
Consider specializing in a niche area within your field. For instance, if you’re a data engineer, try focusing on specific cloud data platforms like Snowflake or Google BigQuery. Specialization can significantly enhance your marketability and appeal.
