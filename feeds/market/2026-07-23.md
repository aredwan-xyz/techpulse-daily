# Developer Market Pulse - July 23, 2026

## 🔥 Hottest Skills Right Now
1. **Generative AI Development**: With the rapid rise of AI-assisted tools, skills in generative models (like those used in text, image, and audio generation) are particularly sought after. Companies are leveraging these to automate content production and enhance customer engagement.
   
2. **Edge Computing**: As IoT devices proliferate, demand for skills in edge computing is on the rise. Developers who can optimize applications for low-latency environments are vital for industries like smart manufacturing and telecommunications.

3. **Natural Language Processing (NLP)**: With the continued interest in AI chatbots and virtual assistants, proficiency in NLP skills is critical. Organizations are looking for developers who can enhance user experiences through advanced conversational interfaces.

4. **Cybersecurity Mesh**: With data breaches becoming more common, skills in cybersecurity mesh architecture are increasingly valued. Developers who can implement a distributed, flexible security approach are in high demand as businesses prioritize security resilience.

5. **Full-stack Development with a Focus on JAMstack**: Knowledge in JavaScript, APIs, and Markup (JAMstack) has become essential due to its efficiency and scalability in web development, leading to a surge in demand for full-stack developers who are adept in this architecture.

## 💰 Salary Signals
- **Average Salary for AI/ML Engineers**: Currently sits at **$135,000/year**, reflecting a healthy 8% increase from last year as companies invest heavily in AI technologies.
  
- **Full-stack Developer Average Salary**: Now at **$120,000/year**, up from $110,000 a year prior, indicating a healthy market as businesses move towards integrated technology solutions.
  
- **Cybersecurity Analysts**: Their average salary has surged to **$115,000**, potentially fueled by heightened security investments due to increasing cyber threats across industries.

## 🚀 Roles in High Demand
1. **Machine Learning Engineer**: Companies are racing to implement AI solutions, creating countless opportunities for ML engineers to design, build, and manage models.
  
2. **DevOps Engineer**: With companies increasingly adopting CI/CD practices, DevOps engineers with cloud and automation skills are vital for streamlining deployment processes.
  
3. **Data Scientist**: Organizations are looking for data experts capable of turning large datasets into actionable insights, leading to increased demand for data scientists who can leverage AI/ML tools.

## 📉 What's Cooling Off
- **Java Developers**: Once a staple in the development world, Java is losing its shine as organizations increasingly favor modern programming languages like Python and JavaScript, leading to decreased demand for Java-specific roles.
  
- **Traditional Web Developers**: As JAMstack continues to rise, traditional web development skills, particularly those based on monolithic architectures, are seeing a decline in opportunities.

## 🌍 Remote Landscape
The remote work model remains robust in tech, with **67% of companies** surveyed indicating they are open to hiring fully remote roles, up 10% from the previous year. However, there is increasing pressure to establish hybrid models to facilitate team collaboration, particularly in roles focused on innovation and brainstorming.

## 💡 Career Move of the Week
Consider enhancing your skills in generative AI by enrolling in a specialized workshop or online course. Given the surging demand for this expertise, positioning yourself as proficient in creating AI-driven solutions could significantly boost your employability.
