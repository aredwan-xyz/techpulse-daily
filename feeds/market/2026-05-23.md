# Developer Market Pulse - May 23, 2026

## 🔥 Hottest Skills Right Now
1. **Generative AI Models**: With companies racing to integrate advanced generative technologies into their products, knowledge of frameworks like OpenAI's GPT-5 and Google's Bard is invaluable right now.
2. **Cloud Native Development**: Proficiency in building cloud-native applications using platforms like AWS Lambda and Kubernetes is essential as firms shift workloads to the cloud.
3. **Data Engineering**: Expertise in tools like Apache Kafka and Snowflake remains crucial as organizations need to handle complex data architectures and real-time analytics.
4. **DevOps Automation**: Skills in CI/CD tools such as Jenkins and GitHub Actions are ever-important as businesses look for ways to automate and streamline their development processes.
5. **Cybersecurity Awareness**: With an uptick in cyber threats, having skills in securing applications and understanding the OWASP Top Ten remains critical for developers across roles.

## 💰 Salary Signals
1. **Average AI Engineer Salary**: As of May 2026, average salaries for AI engineers have surged to approximately $165,000, reflecting a 10% increase over the past year.
2. **Full-Stack Developer Trends**: The average salary for full-stack developers has reached around $145,000, a 8% growth attributed to increased demand for versatile skill sets.
3. **Machine Learning Specialists**: Individuals in machine learning roles are commanding around $160,000 annually, as companies seek to bolster their AI capabilities.

## 🚀 Roles in High Demand
1. **AI/ML Engineer**: With the rise of generative AI applications, there’s a 40% increase in job openings compared to last quarter.
2. **Cloud DevOps Engineer**: Companies are rapidly adopting cloud solutions, leading to a rise in demand for Cloud DevOps positions focused on automation and scaling.
3. **Data Scientist**: The need for actionable insights from massive datasets means data scientists are crucial, with openings up 25% in the last six months.

## 📉 What's Cooling Off
- **Traditional QA Positions**: The role of manual testers is on the decline as more companies adopt test automation practices, leading to a 15% drop in related job openings.
- **Basic Front-End Development**: Demand for basic front-end roles focusing solely on HTML/CSS is waning, giving way to integrated roles that require knowledge of frameworks like React or Vue.js.

## 🌍 Remote Landscape
Remote work remains strong in tech, although there is a noticeable shift towards hybrid models. Approximately 60% of companies are now offering hybrid working arrangements, as teams recognize the value of in-person collaboration while maintaining some flexibility.

## 💡 Career Move of the Week
**Upskill in Generative AI**: If you haven't already, now is the time to invest in learning generative AI frameworks. Consider enrolling in a training program for GPT-5 or similar technologies to enhance your marketability.
