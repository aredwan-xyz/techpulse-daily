# Daily Developer Market Pulse – June 30, 2026

## 🔥 Hottest Skills Right Now
1. **Generative AI Frameworks**: With the rise of tools like OpenAI’s ChatGPT and Google's Bard, expertise in frameworks such as TensorFlow and PyTorch for generative AI applications is skyrocketing.
2. **MLOps**: The integration of machine learning models into production has made MLOps a critical skill. Companies are looking for professionals who can streamline the deployment and monitoring of ML workflows.
3. **API Development**: As organizations emphasize seamless integrations in microservices architectures, proficiency in building and managing RESTful and GraphQL APIs remains crucial.
4. **Cloud Native Development**: Skills in Kubernetes and Docker for managing cloud environments are essential. Companies are migrating to cloud platforms for efficiency.
5. **Data Ethics and Governance**: As privacy regulations tighten, knowledge of ethical AI practices and data governance policies is increasingly sought after.

## 💰 Salary Signals
1. **AI Engineers**: Average salaries have risen to approximately **$150,000** in urban tech hubs, reflecting a 15% increase year-over-year.
2. **Full-Stack Developers**: Salaries for senior full-stack roles are now averaging **$135,000**, driven by the demand for versatile developers who can handle both the backend and frontend effortlessly.
3. **Machine Learning Specialists**: With increasing complexity in ML projects, salaries have surged to around **$160,000**, making this field one of the highest-paying in the tech industry.

## 🚀 Roles in High Demand
1. **AI/ML Engineer**: With a staggering **80%** increase in job postings in the last quarter alone, AI/ML Engineers are critical for developing intelligent systems.
2. **DevOps Engineer**: As companies continue to embrace continuous integration and delivery (CI/CD), DevOps roles have seen **25%** growth, making them essential for effective software development lifecycles.
3. **Data Scientist**: A consistent favorite, the need for data scientists is still high with about **70,000** open positions, as companies leverage data-driven strategies to enhance decision-making.

## 📉 What's Cooling Off
- **Traditional Frontend Frameworks**: Technologies like jQuery are becoming less relevant as modern JavaScript libraries (e.g., React, Vue.js) take precedence.
- **Legacy System Developers**: Roles centered around older technologies (e.g., COBOL, mainframe developers) are seeing a decline in demand as organizations transition to modern platforms.

## 🌍 Remote Landscape
The remote work landscape is stabilizing, with **65%** of tech roles now offering full remote options. However, companies are increasingly emphasizing hybrid models and in-office collaboration days as they seek to build cohesive teams while retaining flexibility.

## 💡 Career Move of the Week
Developers should consider upskilling in **MLOps** this week. Coursera and Udacity are offering tailored courses on the best practices for deploying machine learning models. This skill is critical due to the growing intersection of ML with DevOps practices.
