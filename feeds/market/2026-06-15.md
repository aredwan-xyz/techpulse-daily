# Developer Market Pulse for June 15, 2026

## 🔥 Hottest Skills Right Now
1. **Generative AI Frameworks**: Demand for skills in PyTorch and TensorFlow for building generative AI applications has skyrocketed, driven by the boom in AI-generated content.
2. **Edge Computing**: As IoT devices proliferate, expertise in edge computing technologies, particularly AWS IoT Greengrass and Azure IoT Edge, is increasingly sought after for low-latency applications.
3. **Cloud-Native Development**: Proficiency in Kubernetes and microservices architecture is vital as companies pivot to more agile cloud operations to enhance scalability and efficiency.
4. **Data Privacy Engineering**: With growing regulatory requirements around data protection, skills in implementing privacy-preserving technologies like differential privacy are in high demand.
5. **Quantum Computing Programming**: While still niche, knowledge of quantum programming languages such as Qiskit and Cirq is becoming attractive as organizations explore quantum solutions.

## 💰 Salary Signals
1. **AI Engineers**: Average salaries for AI engineers have reached $150,000, reflecting a 10% increase from last year owing to heightened demand for automation and machine learning models.
2. **Full-stack Developers**: The average salary for full-stack developers is now approximately $120,000, up from $110,000 in 2025, largely due to their critical role in web application development.
3. **Data Scientists**: The average compensation for data scientists has seen a robust increase to $140,000, reflecting a continued need for professionals who can extract insights from complex datasets.

## 🚀 Roles in High Demand
1. **AI Product Manager**: Bridging the gap between technical and business teams, AI product managers are crucial for companies developing AI-driven products, leading to a surge in availability of positions.
2. **DevOps Engineer**: With ongoing transitions to cloud infrastructures and containerization, DevOps engineers remain in high demand as organizations seek to optimize their deployment processes.
3. **Data Engineer**: As companies grapple with vast amounts of data, data engineers skilled in ETL processes and cloud data warehousing tools like Snowflake are increasingly sought after to build scalable data pipelines.

## 📉 What's Cooling Off
1. **Traditional Web Development (HTML/CSS)**: While foundational, skills specific to static web development are increasingly seen as less critical; many developers are expected to integrate frameworks like React or Vue.js.
2. **QA Manual Testing**: As automation tools gain prevalence, manual testing roles have been declining. Companies are now looking for QA engineers who can implement automated testing frameworks.

## 🌍 Remote Landscape
The remote work model for tech jobs remains strong, with approximately 70% of developers reporting fully remote or hybrid work options. However, companies are starting to prioritize in-person collaboration for certain roles, especially those related to team leadership or product management, signaling a potential pivot towards more hybrid models.

## 💡 Career Move of the Week
**Upskill in AI Frameworks**: With the demand for AI skills at an all-time high, consider taking online courses in frameworks like PyTorch or TensorFlow. Completing a project that utilizes these skills can help you stand out in the job market.
