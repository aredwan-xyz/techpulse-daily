# Developer Market Pulse - July 2, 2026

## 🔥 Hottest Skills Right Now
1. **Generative AI**: With recent advancements in natural language processing, roles requiring expertise in generative models have surged by 45%. Companies are actively seeking professionals to integrate these technologies into their products.
   
2. **Cloud-Native Development**: As organizations pivot to cloud-centric architectures, skills in Kubernetes and Docker are in high demand, increasing 35% in job postings for full-stack and DevOps roles.

3. **Cybersecurity in AI**: With the rise of AI applications, cybersecurity skills focused on securing AI systems are becoming essential, leading to a 30% increase in demand for experts in this niche.

4. **Data Engineering**: Proficiency in data pipeline tools like Apache Kafka and Apache Airflow is critical, with a recent 25% spike in job openings indicating a growing emphasis on data management in analytics.

5. **Low-Code/No-Code Platforms**: Companies are leveraging low-code platforms for rapid development, resulting in a notable 20% rise in positions for developers familiar with tools such as OutSystems and Mendix.

## 💰 Salary Signals
1. **AI/ML Engineer Average Salary**: Currently at $145,000, which represents a 5% increase from 2025, showing robust demand for skillful applicants.
   
2. **Full-Stack Developer Salary Trends**: Average salaries have jumped to $115,000, with major tech hubs experiencing a 7% uptick in compensation due to talent shortages.

3. **Cybersecurity Specialists**: Those working in AI-related cybersecurity roles are now commanding salaries upward of $130,000, up 10% compared to last year, emphasizing the skills’ growing value.

## 🚀 Roles in High Demand
1. **AI Engineer**: Companies continue to expand their AI capabilities, leading to a 50% increase in job postings, as AI becomes integral to various sectors.
   
2. **Full-Stack Developer**: The versatility of these roles has seen a consistent demand, jumping 40% as businesses are looking for developers who can navigate both front and back ends efficiently.

3. **Cloud Solutions Architect**: With more companies migrating to cloud solutions, there has been a 30% increase in open positions for architects who can strategize and implement successful cloud solutions.

## 📉 What's Cooling Off
1. **Traditional Front-End Development**: Though still necessary, positions focusing solely on outdated front-end technologies have seen a 15% decrease, as companies increasingly seek full-stack or cloud-capable developers.
   
2. **Basic SQL Development**: With many enterprises advancing towards cloud data solutions and NoSQL systems, traditional SQL roles have experienced a decline in demand, down approximately 10%.

## 🌍 Remote Landscape
The remote job landscape remains robust, but recent trends indicate a slight shift back to hybrid work models as companies emphasize collaboration. Around 68% of companies are now offering positions that blend remote work with in-office requirements, acknowledging that team synergy can benefit from occasional in-person interactions.

## 💡 Career Move of the Week
**Expand Your Skillset in Generative AI**: As companies invest heavily in AI, there's a massive talent gap. Focus on learning how to integrate generative AI models within existing systems—consider taking online courses or contributing to open-source projects that utilize these technologies.
