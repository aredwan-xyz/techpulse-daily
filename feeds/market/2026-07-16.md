# Tech Job Market Pulse - July 16, 2026

## 🔥 Hottest Skills Right Now
1. **Generative AI**: The demand for developers skilled in generative AI has surged by 45% in just the last quarter, as businesses look to harness tools like GPT-4 and beyond for content generation and automation.
2. **Cloud-native Development**: With a 35% increase in job postings, expertise in cloud platforms such as AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud remains vital for full-stack developers who can leverage these technologies for scalable applications.
3. **Data Engineering**: As organizations invest heavily in big data analytics, data engineering skills have become a top priority, with a 30% rise in demand, particularly for professionals adept with tools like Apache Spark and Kafka.
4. **Cybersecurity in AI**: There is an increasing need (up 50%) for developers who can integrate cybersecurity measures into AI systems, a response to growing concerns over AI vulnerabilities.
5. **Low-Code Development**: Job postings in low-code platforms have jumped by 28% as companies seek developers who can expedite project timelines without sacrificing quality.

## 💰 Salary Signals
1. **AI/ML Engineers**: Current salaries have reached a median of $135,000, reflecting a 10% increase from last year.
2. **Full-stack Developers**: The average compensation stands at $120,000, with top talent earning upwards of $150,000 particularly in tech hubs.
3. **Cloud Architects**: Salaries have seen a significant jump to an average of $145,000, driven by the ongoing digital transformation in enterprises.

## 🚀 Roles in High Demand
1. **Machine Learning Engineer**: With a staggering 18,000 open positions, organizations urgently need professionals who can develop and deploy ML models across various sectors.
2. **DevOps Engineer**: The rise in remote infrastructure management and CI/CD practices has led to a demand for over 15,000 DevOps roles, as companies strive for more agile development practices.
3. **Data Scientist**: 12,000 vacancies are reported for data scientists, with a notable focus on candidates familiar with both statistical analysis and machine learning.

## 📉 What's Cooling Off
- **Blockchain Developers**: After years of explosive growth, specific roles in blockchain technology have softened by about 20%, with fewer enterprises investing in private blockchain solutions.
- **Mobile App Developers (Native)**: As cross-platform solutions thrive, demand for native mobile app developers has declined by roughly 15%, indicating a shift towards hybrid frameworks.

## 🌍 Remote Landscape
The state of remote work in tech remains robust, with 56% of job postings indicating some form of remote flexibility. Companies are increasingly comfortable with remote teams, and many have adopted hybrid models, providing candidates with more geographical flexibility.

## 💡 Career Move of the Week
Consider enhancing your skill set by delving into **Machine Learning Operations (MLOps)**. This area is growing rapidly, as companies seek to simplify and scale their ML workflows. Online courses focusing on MLOps tools and frameworks can significantly increase your employability.
