# Developer Market Pulse - June 22, 2026

## 🔥 Hottest Skills Right Now
1. **Machine Learning Operations (MLOps)**: As organizations move towards scalable AI solutions, MLOps skills are critical for deploying machine learning models efficiently. Companies are looking for DevOps engineers who can bridge the gap between development and operations in AI projects.
   
2. **Natural Language Processing (NLP)**: With the rise of AI chatbots and virtual assistants, proficiency in NLP is becoming increasingly sought after, especially in industries like customer service, healthcare, and finance where automated communication is key.
   
3. **Full-Stack Development (JavaScript frameworks)**: Full-stack developers adept in modern JavaScript frameworks like React, Vue.js, and Node.js are in high demand as companies push for rapid web application development to enhance user experience.
   
4. **Data Engineering**: As businesses focus on data-driven decision-making, data engineers are crucial for building robust data pipelines and ensuring data quality. The demand for ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) skills is particularly high.
   
5. **Cybersecurity (Cloud Security)**: With the continuing trend towards remote architectures and Cloud services, expertise in securing cloud environments is critical. Professionals with skills in cloud security are receiving greater attention from hiring managers.

## 💰 Salary Signals
- **AI/ML Engineer Average Salary**: The median salary for AI/ML engineers has risen to $145,000, a 10% increase since last year.
- **Full-stack Developer Compensation**: Full-stack developers are earning an average of $120,000, with top talent in major tech hubs earning up to $180,000 depending on experience and skill set.
- **Data Engineer Salary Trends**: Data engineers can expect a median salary of $130,000, with senior positions enjoying compensation packages upwards of $160,000.

## 🚀 Roles in High Demand
1. **AI/ML Engineer**: With businesses incorporating AI solutions into their operations, the need for AI/ML engineers has surged. Companies like Google and Amazon are actively hiring talent.
   
2. **Full-Stack Developer**: Given the rise in web applications, there are over 15,000 open positions nationwide for full-stack developers, particularly for those with JavaScript expertise.
   
3. **Data Engineer**: As organizations increasingly rely on data analytics, the demand for data engineers is explosive, with around 12,000 openings at the moment, especially in finance and tech.

## 📉 What's Cooling Off
- **Mobile Development**: The demand for mobile app developers is seeing a decline as many companies are shifting their focus to web and enterprise applications, driven by more engagement on desktop and web apps than mobile.
- **Legacy Systems**: Roles demanding expertise in outdated programming languages and systems, such as COBOL or older enterprise solutions, are increasingly less sought after.

## 🌍 Remote Landscape
Remote work remains strong in the tech industry, with about 60% of developers still opting for fully remote positions. Flexibility is a key consideration, and many companies are now promoting hybrid models for better work-life balance. There has been a noticeable increase in remote roles that allow for international candidates, expanding the talent pool for employers.

## 💡 Career Move of the Week
Consider upskilling in MLOps methodologies. Familiarizing yourself with tools like Kubernetes for model deployment and monitoring can significantly increase your attractiveness to employers looking for talent that can streamline their AI deployments.

## 📚 Today's Learning Pick

**Resource:** [TensorFlow Playground](http://playground.tensorflow.org)

**Why today:** As machine learning and AI continue to evolve, understanding the basics of neural networks is essential for any developer in this space. The TensorFlow Playground offers a hands-on experience to grasp the concepts of neural networks in a visually engaging manner, making complex ideas more approachable.

**What you'll learn:**
- **Neural Network Configuration:** How to configure different layers, activation functions, and neurons to observe their impact on learning.
- **Overfitting vs. Underfitting:** Understand the balance between complexity and performance, and how to recognize when a model is too simple or too complex.
- **Data Visualization:** Gain insights into how data distributions affect model performance and learn to interpret visual feedback as you tweak parameters.

**Time investment:** Approximately 30-60 minutes for a focused session, but you can return multiple times to explore different configurations and datasets.

**Best for:** Beginners and intermediate developers interested in AI and machine learning, as well as educators looking for a tool to explain neural networks interactively.
