# 🛠️ Developer Market Pulse - June 23, 2026

## 🔥 Hottest Skills Right Now
1. **Generative AI**: With the rapid advent of AI-driven creativity tools, skills in generative AI technologies (like GPT-4/5 frameworks) are seeing explosive demand among developers looking to create innovative applications.
2. **Data Privacy and Ethics in AI**: As regulations tighten around data use, expertise in data privacy has become critical. Developers with skills in compliance frameworks and ethical AI deployment are highly sought after.
3. **Cloud-Native Development**: Proficiency in cloud platforms (AWS, Azure, GCP) and related services (Kubernetes, Docker) remains indispensable for building scalable applications, particularly for companies increasing their remote and hybrid operational models.
4. **Low-Code/No-Code Platforms**: The rising trend of enabling non-developers to create applications has brought low-code solutions to the forefront. Familiarity with platforms like Salesforce and Mendix can be advantageous.
5. **DevOps Practices**: Knowledge in DevOps, CI/CD pipelines, and automation tools continues to be vital for organizations aiming to enhance their development lifecycles and improve deployment speed.

## 💰 Salary Signals
1. **AI/ML Engineers**: Average salaries have risen to $130,000, marking a 10% increase year-over-year as companies compete for top talent in this competitive space.
2. **Full-Stack Developers**: Demand for versatile developers is pushing salaries to new heights, with the average compensation rising to $115,000, up from $105,000 last year.
3. **Data Scientists**: This field remains lucrative with salaries averaging $125,000, reflecting a consistent growth trend as businesses implement more data-driven decision-making strategies.

## 🚀 Roles in High Demand
1. **AI/ML Engineer**: The surge in AI applications is resulting in a sharp boost in job openings for AI/ML engineers, with over 20,000 positions listed nationwide. Companies are investing heavily in AI capabilities, requiring specialized talent.
2. **Full-Stack Developer**: With businesses continuing to evolve their online presence, over 15,000 full-stack developer roles are currently open, showcasing the need for professionals skilled in both front-end and back-end technologies.
3. **Data Engineer**: With organizations collecting more data than ever, the demand for skilled data engineers has rocketed to nearly 12,000 open roles, as data pipelines and integration skills become crucial.

## 📉 What's Cooling Off
- **Legacy Systems Development**: Roles and skills centered around outdated technologies (e.g., COBOL, VB.NET) are declining significantly, with many jobs being phased out as companies modernize their tech stacks.
- **Heatmaps and Basic Analytics**: Standard analytics roles focusing solely on basic data visualization tools (like simple Excel reports) are also losing traction, as firms seek deeper insights and automation.

## 🌍 Remote Landscape
The remote work trend continues to solidify, with around 60% of tech jobs listed as fully remote. However, many companies are beginning to adopt hybrid work models, leading to demand for candidates willing to work in flexible arrangements. There's an increase in companies offering stipends for home office setups, influencing job attractiveness.

## 💡 Career Move of the Week
Invest in upskilling in generative AI frameworks. Resources such as online courses from platforms like Coursera and Udacity focusing on the latest in AI tech can significantly enhance employability and open doors to high-paying roles.
