# Daily Market Pulse for Developers – Tuesday, June 16, 2026

## 🔥 Hottest Skills Right Now
1. **Machine Learning Operations (MLOps)**: As companies increasingly adopt ML models into production, the demand for skill sets that integrate machine learning and operations is surging. MLOps expertise facilitates model monitoring and governance, making it a vital knowledge area.
  
2. **Observability in Cloud-Native Applications**: As developers build more complex applications on microservices architectures, understanding observability tools like Prometheus and Grafana is essential. Companies are searching for professionals who can ensure reliability and performance in these environments.

3. **Blockchain Development**: Beyond cryptocurrencies, blockchain technology is gaining traction in sectors like finance and supply chain. Skills in smart contract development and DApp creation are significantly in demand.

4. **DevSecOps**: With the increasing importance of security in software development, roles requiring knowledge of DevSecOps practices—integrating security into CI/CD pipelines—are seeing rapid growth.

5. **Edge Computing**: As IoT devices proliferate and latency requirements tighten, developers who can create applications leveraging edge computing principles are highly sought after to improve processing speeds and resource utilization.

## 💰 Salary Signals
1. **AI Engineering**: Average salaries for AI engineers have risen to $140,000, marking a 10% increase from last year, driven by demand for advanced AI solutions.
  
2. **Full-Stack Developers**: Current compensation for full-stack developers now averages $115,000, a slight uptick of 5%, indicating increased value placed on versatile skill sets that bridge frontend and backend.

3. **MLOps Specialists**: These professionals are experiencing a significant pay increase, with average salaries now around $150,000, reflecting a market that increasingly values the operationalization of AI/ML tools.

## 🚀 Roles in High Demand
1. **Data Scientist**: With open positions exceeding 25,000, data scientists are crucial for leveraging data insights for business decisions—particularly in industries like healthcare and finance.

2. **AI Engineer**: The demand for AI engineers has skyrocketed, with 30,000 vacancies, as companies look to implement AI initiatives to stay competitive.

3. **Cloud Security Engineer**: With over 20,000 job openings, the rise in cloud adoption has led businesses to prioritize cybersecurity, leading to an increase in demand for specialists in this area.

## 📉 What's Cooling Off
- **Traditional System Administrators**: Roles traditionally focused on on-premises server management are declining in demand as more businesses migrate to cloud environments.
  
- **Frontend Development with Legacy Frameworks**: Positions requiring skills in older frameworks such as AngularJS are falling out of favor, as modern frameworks like React, Vue.js, and Svelte take precedence.

## 🌍 Remote Landscape
The remote work landscape remains robust, with around 55% of tech roles now offered remotely. Larger organizations are adopting hybrid models, allowing for flexibility while encouraging in-person collaboration at least a few days a week. Contrarily, smaller startups continue to embrace fully remote approaches.

## 💡 Career Move of the Week
**Upskill in MLOps**: If you're looking to enhance your marketability, consider enrolling in courses focused on MLOps. This is a critical area where skill gaps exist, and building expertise here can set you apart from the competition, leading to roles with higher pay and greater job security.
