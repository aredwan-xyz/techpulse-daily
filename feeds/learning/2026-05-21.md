**Topic:** System design at scale
**Day #141**

RESOURCE_TITLE: Designing Data-Intensive Applications
RESOURCE_URL: https://dataintensive.net/
RESOURCE_SOURCE: O'Reilly Media

WHY_NOW:
As organizations increasingly rely on data-driven decision-making, understanding system design at scale has never been more critical. With the push towards cloud computing and microservices, mastering the principles of data-intensive applications prepares developers to build resilient, scalable systems.

WHAT_YOULL_LEARN:
- The fundamentals of system architecture and design principles for scalable applications.
- Techniques for managing data storage, consistency, availability, and partitioning.
- Patterns for building microservices and understanding their architecture implications.
- Strategies for designing fault-tolerant systems that can recover from failures gracefully.

WHO_ITS_FOR:
This resource is ideal for software engineers, system architects, and technical leads who want to deepen their understanding of scalable system design. Whether you're new to system design or looking to refine your existing skills, this content will provide valuable insights.

TIME_INVESTMENT: Approximately 4-6 hours for thorough reading and understanding.

BEST_QUOTE_OR_HIGHLIGHT:
"Thinking about the system design is not just about architecture but understanding how to manage complexity over time and through unexpected challenges."

NEXT_STEP:
After finishing this resource, apply your newfound knowledge by sketching out a scalable system design for a hypothetical application, incorporating the principles and patterns learned.
