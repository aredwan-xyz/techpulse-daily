PROMPT_TITLE: Crafting Clear Technical Emails

THE_PROMPT:
Subject: [Subject line that summarizes the email clearly]

Hi [Recipient's Name],

I hope this message finds you well. I'm writing to discuss [briefly introduce the main topic or purpose of the email]. 

[Provide a detailed explanation or background information about the topic. Use bullet points if necessary for clarity, such as:]
- [Key point or issue 1]
- [Key point or issue 2]
- [Key point or issue 3]

To address this, I propose [outline your suggestion or request clearly]. Additionally, please find the attached document [if applicable] that includes [brief description of the attachment].

Could we schedule a call or meeting to discuss this further? Please let me know your availability for [suggest a few dates/times].

Thank you for your attention to this matter. Looking forward to your response.

Best regards,  
[Your Name]  
[Your Position]  
[Your Company]  
[Your Contact Information]

WHEN_TO_USE:
Use this template when you need to communicate technical information or requests to colleagues, clients, or partners in a professional setting. It's ideal for ensuring clarity and professionalism in your technical correspondence.

EXAMPLE_INPUT:
- Subject: Update on Software Development Timeline
- Recipient's Name: John Doe
- Main topic: the progress and next steps for our development project
- Key point or issue 1: Completion of Phase 1 is on schedule
- Key point or issue 2: Testing phase to start next week
- Key point or issue 3: Resource allocation for Phase 2
- Suggestion: a meeting to align on timelines and deliverables
- Availability: next Tuesday or Thursday afternoon

EXAMPLE_OUTPUT_SUMMARY:
This prompt consistently generates clear and structured technical emails that effectively communicate important information, making it easy for the recipient to understand the issue and respond accordingly. The tone is professional, and the layout ensures that key points are highlighted for quick reference.

PRO_TIPS:
- Always tailor the subject line to be as descriptive as possible.
- Use bullet points for better readability, especially with complex information.
- Make sure to follow up if you don't receive a response within a few days.
