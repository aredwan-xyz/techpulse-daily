PROMPT_TITLE: Define Your Product Vision

THE_PROMPT:
You are tasked with writing clear product requirements for a new product. Please provide the following details:
1. **Product Name**: {PRODUCT_NAME}
2. **Overview**: A brief description of what the product does and its primary purpose. {OVERVIEW}
3. **Target Audience**: Who will use this product? Be specific about demographics, needs, and pain points. {TARGET_AUDIENCE}
4. **Key Features**: List at least three core features that the product must have. Be concise but descriptive. {KEY_FEATURES}
5. **User Experience**: Describe the ideal user journey when interacting with the product. Include any key interactions or touchpoints. {USER_EXPERIENCE}
6. **Success Metrics**: What metrics will define the success of this product? List quantitative and qualitative measures. {SUCCESS_METRICS}

WHEN_TO_USE:
Use this prompt when you're developing a new product or feature and need to clarify its requirements and communicate your vision effectively to stakeholders. It ensures all aspects of the product are covered comprehensively.

EXAMPLE_INPUT:
PRODUCT_NAME: Smart Water Bottle  
OVERVIEW: A reusable water bottle that tracks hydration levels through a connected app.  
TARGET_AUDIENCE: Health-conscious individuals aged 18-35 who prioritize fitness and wellness.  
KEY_FEATURES: 1) Real-time hydration tracking, 2) Mobile app integration, 3) Temperature control.  
USER_EXPERIENCE: Users will receive notifications via the app when they need to drink water, adjusting reminders based on activity levels.  
SUCCESS_METRICS: At least 10,000 bottles sold in the first year; 80% user satisfaction rating.

EXAMPLE_OUTPUT_SUMMARY:
This prompt reliably produces a structured and clear product specification document that outlines essential details, ensuring a focused approach in product development. It helps to standardize communication, making it easier for teams to align on objectives and deliverables.

PRO_TIPS:
- Be as specific as possible when filling in the placeholders to reduce ambiguity in requirements.
- Think about the user perspective, focusing on what they truly need from the product.
- Ensure that success metrics are actionable and easily measurable to monitor progress over time.
